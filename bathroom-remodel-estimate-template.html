What to Include in a Bathroom Remodel Estimate

  • Demolition — removal of existing tile, fixtures, vanity, drywall, and flooring; dumpster or haul-away fees
  • Plumbing — rough-in for shower valve, tub filler, toilet, vanity drain, supply lines; specify if relocating any fixtures
  • Electrical — GFCI outlets, exhaust fan, recessed lights, heated floor wiring, and any panel work
  • Waterproofing — shower pan membrane or liquid-applied waterproofing, cement board, Kerdi or similar systems
  • Tile and flooring — floor tile, wall tile, shower tile, mosaic accents, grout, thinset, and labor per square foot
  • Fixtures — toilet, vanity and top, faucets, shower valve and trim, showerhead, towel bars, mirrors
  • Glass enclosure — frameless door, semi-frameless, or shower curtain rod; specify dimensions
  • Paint and trim — ceiling and wall paint, baseboards, door casing, any wainscoting
  • Permits — building permit, plumbing permit, electrical permit as applicable

Common Bathroom Remodel Projects to Quote

Full Gut Renovation

Strip to studs and rebuild. Include demo, framing repairs, plumbing rough-in, electrical rough-in, waterproofing, tile, fixtures, glass, paint, and trim. These projects ($15,000-$35,000+) require the most detailed estimates because there are so many line items and the potential for change orders is high.

Tub-to-Shower Conversion

Remove bathtub and install a walk-in shower with new tile, valve, glass enclosure, and niche. Include plumbing modifications for the new drain location, waterproofing, and any structural work needed for the curb or curbless entry. Typical range: $5,000-$12,000.

Vanity and Fixture Update

Replace vanity, countertop, faucet, mirror, and light fixture without touching tile or the shower. A cosmetic refresh that runs $2,000-$5,000. Even on smaller jobs, a professional estimate sets you apart from the handyman who texts a number.

Tile Refresh

Remove old tile and install new floor and wall tile. Include demo, cement board if needed, waterproofing in wet areas, tile, grout, and labor. Quote tile labor by the square foot and list material costs separately so the customer can adjust their tile selection without a whole new estimate.

Accessibility / Aging-in-Place Remodel

Barrier-free shower, grab bars, comfort-height toilet, wider doorway, non-slip tile. These specialized projects serve a growing market and often qualify for VA or insurance funding. Detail each accessibility feature as a separate line item.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should a bathroom remodel estimate include?

A thorough bathroom remodel estimate should include demolition, plumbing rough-in, electrical rough-in, waterproofing, tile and flooring, fixtures (toilet, vanity, faucets, shower valve), glass enclosure or shower door, paint, trim, accessories, permits, and labor broken down by trade. Include allowances for fixtures if the homeowner hasn't selected them yet.

How much does a bathroom remodel typically cost?

A standard bathroom remodel ranges from $10,000-$25,000 depending on size and scope. A tub-to-shower conversion runs $5,000-$12,000. A full gut renovation of a master bathroom can exceed $30,000. Your estimate should break down materials and labor for each phase so the homeowner understands the investment.

Should I use allowances or fixed prices for fixtures?

Use allowances when the homeowner hasn't selected fixtures yet. For example, include a $500 allowance for the toilet and a $1,200 allowance for the vanity. This gives a realistic total while leaving room for the customer to upgrade or downgrade. Once selections are made, update with exact prices.
