What to Include in an AC Installation Proposal

  • Equipment specifications — brand, model number, tonnage, SEER2 rating, and refrigerant type (R-410A or R-32)
  • Materials — copper line set, thermostat (programmable or smart), electrical disconnect, condenser pad, refrigerant
  • Labor — installation hours, electrical work, brazing, startup and commissioning
  • Permits and fees — mechanical permit, inspection fees, disposal of old equipment
  • Scope of work — what's included and what's not (e.g., ductwork modifications, electrical panel upgrades)
  • Warranty terms — manufacturer warranty, workmanship guarantee, parts vs. labor coverage
  • Payment terms — deposit amount, payment schedule, accepted methods

Common AC Installation Jobs to Quote

Straight AC Replacement (Like-for-Like)

The most common job: replacing an existing condenser and sometimes the evaporator coil with a new unit of the same capacity. Include removal and disposal of the old unit, new line set if needed, and startup/commissioning. Typical residential quotes range $3,500-$6,000.

Full Split System Installation

New condenser plus new air handler or furnace-coil combination. Include line set, thermostat upgrade, electrical work, and permits. These larger jobs ($5,000-$10,000+) benefit the most from a professional proposal since the customer is spending significant money.

Ductless Mini-Split Systems

Single-zone or multi-zone mini-split installations for additions, garages, or homes without ductwork. Quote the outdoor unit, indoor head(s), line set covers, electrical, and mounting hardware. Be specific about the number of zones and BTU capacity per head.

AC System Add-On (No Existing Cooling)

Adding air conditioning to a home that only has heating. These projects often include the condenser, A-coil, line set, thermostat, electrical circuit, and sometimes ductwork modifications. Clearly outline what ductwork is and isn't included in your scope.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should an AC installation proposal include?

A professional AC installation proposal should include equipment specifications (brand, model, tonnage, SEER2 rating), all materials (line set, thermostat, disconnect, pad), labor costs, permit fees, old unit disposal, warranty terms, scope of work, and payment terms. How much does an AC installation typically cost to quote?

Residential AC installations typically range from $3,500 to $7,500 for a straight replacement, and $5,000 to $12,000+ for a full system with ductwork modifications. Your proposal should break down equipment, labor, materials, and permits so the homeowner sees exactly where their money goes.
