What to Include in a Water Heater Estimate

  • Water heater unit — brand, model, capacity (gallons or GPM), fuel type (gas, electric, or hybrid), energy factor
  • Expansion tank — required by code in most areas; include it as a line item even if it's already present
  • Plumbing connections — supply lines, shut-off valves, dielectric unions, drain pan
  • Gas or electrical work — gas flex connector, sediment trap, or new electrical circuit for electric/hybrid units
  • Venting — B-vent for atmospheric, stainless steel for power vent/tankless, or PVC for condensing units
  • Permits and disposal — plumbing permit, inspection fee, haul-away and disposal of old unit
  • Warranty and terms — manufacturer warranty length, your workmanship guarantee, payment terms

Water Heater Jobs You Can Quote

Standard Tank Replacement (40-50 Gallon)

The bread-and-butter plumbing job. Remove the old tank, install a new one in the same location with new supply lines, gas flex, and expansion tank. Typical residential quotes range $1,200-$2,500. Include disposal fees and permit costs to avoid surprises.

Tankless Water Heater Upgrade

Converting from tank to tankless requires additional work: larger gas line (often 3/4" to 1"), new venting (stainless steel or PVC), electrical outlet for the control board, and sometimes a recirculation pump. Quote each modification as a separate line item so the customer understands the higher price ($3,000-$5,500).

Electric to Gas Conversion

Running a new gas line, adding venting, and removing the old electric circuit. These jobs need both a plumbing and gas permit in most areas. Detail every modification in the scope of work since the customer may not understand why the conversion costs more than a straight swap.

Heat Pump (Hybrid) Water Heater

Increasingly popular for energy savings. These units need a 240V circuit, adequate airspace (typically 750+ cubic feet), and a condensate drain. Include the higher unit cost but mention the energy savings and potential utility rebates in your scope of work.

Commercial Water Heater Installation

Larger units (75-100+ gallons) or multiple tankless units for commercial properties. Include recirculation systems, mixing valves, seismic strapping if required, and commercial permit fees. These higher-value jobs especially benefit from professional proposals.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should a water heater replacement estimate include?

A complete water heater estimate should include the unit cost (brand, model, gallon capacity or GPM for tankless), removal and disposal of the old unit, plumbing connections, gas line or electrical work, expansion tank if required by code, permit fees, and labor. Include warranty details for both the unit and your workmanship.

How much does a water heater replacement cost?

A standard 40-50 gallon tank water heater replacement typically costs $1,200-$2,500 installed. Tankless water heater installations range from $3,000-$5,500 depending on whether gas line upgrades or new venting is needed. Your estimate should clearly break down these costs so the homeowner can compare options.

Should I offer tank vs. tankless options in the same estimate?

Yes — offering both options in a single proposal is a great sales technique. Create two sections: one for a standard tank replacement and one for a tankless upgrade. This lets the homeowner see the price difference and often results in upselling to the tankless option.

What permits are needed for water heater replacement?

Most jurisdictions require a plumbing permit for water heater replacement, and some also require a gas permit or electrical permit depending on the fuel type. Include permit fees as a line item in your estimate so the customer knows the full cost upfront. This also signals that you do the job by the book.
